**Answer – A Hoare‑logic proof that the Python program satisfies the specification**  

We prove the total‑correctness Hoare triple  

\[
\{P\}\;\texttt{Program}\;\{Q\}
\]

where  

* \(P\) is the pre‑condition (3)  

\[
P\equiv s\in\mathbb R_{2}\land s\ge 0\land\text{the input stream contains exactly the token }s .
\]

* \(Q\) is the post‑condition (4)  

\[
\begin{aligned}
Q\equiv &\;\exists p\in\{4,7,10,12,15\}\,
          \bigl(p=\operatorname{perc}(s)\bigr)                     \\
        &\land\;\operatorname{inc}(s)=\tfrac{p}{100}s               \\
        &\land\;\operatorname{new}(s)=s+\operatorname{inc}(s)       \\
        &\land\;{\tt out}= \bigl(\text{“Novo salario: ”}\Vert\texttt{fmt}(\operatorname{new}(s),2)\bigr)\\
        &\qquad\Vert\bigl(\text{“Reajuste ganho: ”}\Vert\texttt{fmt}(\operatorname{inc}(s),2)\bigr)\\
        &\qquad\Vert\bigl(\text{“Em percentual: ”}\Vert p\Vert\text{ “ %”}\bigr) .
\end{aligned}
\]

The proof proceeds by annotating the program with **assertions** (the *intermediate* Hoare
states) and applying the standard Hoare inference rules:

* **(Assign)** \(\{Q[e/x]\}\;x:=e\;\{Q\}\)  
* **(If‑Then‑Else)** \(\displaystyle\frac{\{P\land B\}\;C_{1}\;\{Q\}\qquad\{P\land\neg B\}\;C_{2}\;\{Q\}}{\{P\}\;\texttt{if }B\texttt{ then }C_{1}\texttt{ else }C_{2}\;\{Q\}}\)  
* **(Seq)** \(\displaystyle\frac{\{P\}\;C_{1}\;\{R\}\qquad\{R\}\;C_{2}\;\{Q\}}{\{P\}\;C_{1};C_{2}\;\{Q\}}\)  
* **(Consequence)** \(\displaystyle\frac{P'\Rightarrow P\qquad\{P\}\;C\;\{Q\}\qquad Q\Rightarrow Q'}{\{P'\}\;C\;\{Q'\}}\)  

We also use the **total‑correctness** rule for `while`/`if` blocks (termination of the
`if‑elif‑else` chain is obvious because the guards are mutually exclusive and cover
all real numbers).

### 2.  Proof of each fragment

#### 2.1  Input and initialisation  

* **(Assign)** on line 1  

\[
\{P\}\;\texttt{salario}=float(input())\;\{s=\texttt{salario}\land P\}=A_{1}.
\]

* **(Assign)** on lines 2‑4  

\[
\{A_{1}\}\;\texttt{percentual}=0\;\{A_{2}\},\qquad
\{A_{2}\}\;\texttt{novoSalario}=0\;\{A_{3}\},\qquad
\{A_{3}\}\;\texttt{reajuste}=0\;\{A_{4}\}.
\]

All three assignments are simple; the rule *Consequence* is used to keep the
previous facts unchanged.

#### 2.2  The `if‑elif‑else` chain  

Define the Boolean guards

\[
\begin{aligned}
B_{1}&\equiv \texttt{salario}\le 400,\\
B_{2}&\equiv \neg B_{1}\wedge \texttt{salario}\le 800,\\
B_{3}&\equiv \neg(B_{1}\lor B_{2})\wedge \texttt{salario}\le 1200,\\
B_{4}&\equiv \neg(B_{1}\lor B_{2}\lor B_{3})\wedge \texttt{salario}\le 2000,\\
B_{5}&\equiv \neg(B_{1}\lor B_{2}\lor B_{3}\lor B_{4}).
\end{aligned}
\]

Observe that the guards are **mutually exclusive** and **exhaustive**:

\[
\bigvee_{i=1}^{5}B_{i}\equiv\texttt{true},\qquad
\bigwedge_{i\neq j}\neg(B_{i}\land B_{j}).
\]

For each branch we prove the Hoare triple that after executing the assignment
`percentual = c` the variable `percentual` holds the value required by
\(\operatorname{perc}(s)\).

*Branch 1* (\(B_{1}\))  

\[
\{A_{4}\land B_{1}\}\;\texttt{percentual}=0.15\;\{A_{5a}\},
\qquad A_{5a}\equiv A_{4}\land \texttt{percentual}=0.15 .
\]

Because \(B_{1}\) is exactly the condition “\(0\le s\le 400\)”, the
definition (1) gives \(\operatorname{perc}(s)=15\), i.e.  

\[
\texttt{percentual}=0.15\;\Longleftrightarrow\;
\texttt{percentual}= \frac{\operatorname{perc}(s)}{100}.
\]

Thus from \(A_{5a}\) we can derive  

\[
A_{5a}\;\Rightarrow\;p=15\land \texttt{percentual}=p/100 .
\]

The same reasoning applies to the other four branches:

| branch | guard            | assignment | value of `percentual` | corresponding \(p\) |
|-------|------------------|------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| 2     | \(B_{2}\)        | `=0.12`    | \(0.12\)             | \(p=12\)            |
| 3     | \(B_{3}\)        | `=0.10`    | \(0.10\)             | \(p=10\)            |
| 4     | \(B_{4}\)        | `=0.07`    | \(0.07\)             | \(p=7\)             |
| 5     | \(B_{5}\)        | `=0.04`    | \(0.04\)             | \(p=4\)             |

Hence, using the **If‑Then‑Else** rule we obtain

\[
\{A_{4}\}\;\texttt{if }B_{1}\texttt{ … elif }B_{5}\texttt{ … else …}\;\{A_{5i}\},
\]

where \(A_{5i}\) is the common assertion  

\[
A_{5i}\equiv A_{4}\land \exists p\in\{4,7,10,12,15\}\;(p=\operatorname{perc}(s)\land
\texttt{percentual}=p/100).
\tag{5}
\]

Thus after the conditional we know the exact percentage \(p\) and we have stored
the *fraction* \(p/100\) in the variable `percentual`.

#### 2.3  Computing the increase and the new salary  

*Line 6* – assignment `novoSalario = salario + salario*percentual`

\[
\{A_{5i}\}\;\texttt{novoSalario}= \texttt{salario}+ \texttt{salario}*\texttt{percentual}\;
\{A_{6}\},
\]

where  

\[
A_{6}\equiv A_{5i}\land
\texttt{novoSalario}= s + s\cdot\frac{p}{100}.
\tag{6}
\]

*Line 7* – assignment `reajuste = salario*percentual`

\[
\{A_{6}\}\;\texttt{reajuste}= \texttt{salario}*\texttt{percentual}\;
\{A_{7}\},
\]

with  

\[
A_{7}\equiv A_{6}\land
\texttt{reajuste}= s\cdot\frac{p}{100}.
\tag{7}
\]

Using (2) we recognise  

\[
\texttt{reajuste}= \operatorname{inc}(s),\qquad
\texttt{novoSalario}= \operatorname{new}(s).
\]

Thus \(A_{7}\) is exactly  

\[
\exists p\bigl(p=\operatorname{perc}(s)\land
\operatorname{inc}(s)=\tfrac{p}{100}s\land
\operatorname{new}(s)=s+\operatorname{inc}(s)\bigr).
\tag{8}
\]

#### 2.4  Printing  

The three `print` statements do not modify program variables, therefore the
usual **(Skip)** rule tells us that the assertions are unchanged; we only have to
show that the *observable output* that is produced matches the textual
description in \(Q\).

*Line 8* prints the string  

\[
\texttt{"Novo salario: "}\;\Vert\;\texttt{format}(\texttt{novoSalario},2).
\]

Because `novoSalario` equals \(\operatorname{new}(s)\) (by (8)) and the
formatting routine rounds to exactly two decimal digits, the first line of
`out` is precisely the first component required by \(Q\).

*Line 9* similarly prints  

\[
\texttt{"Reajuste ganho: "}\;\Vert\;\texttt{format}(\texttt{reajuste},2)
   =\texttt{"Reajuste ganho: "}\;\Vert\;\texttt{format}(\operatorname{inc}(s),2).
\]

*Line 10* prints  

\[
\texttt{"Em percentual: "}\;\Vert\;( \texttt{percentual}*100 )\;\Vert\;\texttt{" %"}.
\]

Since `percentual = p/100`, we have `percentual*100 = p`.  The format specifier
`.0f` removes the fractional part, so the printed integer is exactly the
percentage \(p\).  Consequently the third line of `out` coincides with the last
component of \(Q\).

Collecting the three lines we obtain exactly the string described in the
post‑condition (4).  Hence after line 10 we have

\[
A_{10}\equiv Q .
\]

### 3.  Composition of the whole proof

Putting the pieces together with the **Seq** rule:

\[
\begin{aligned}
&\{P\}\;\texttt{salario}=float(input())\;\{A_{1}\}\\
&\{A_{1}\}\;\texttt{percentual}=0\;\{A_{2}\}\\
&\{A_{2}\}\;\texttt{novoSalario}=0\;\{A_{3}\}\\
&\{A_{3}\}\;\texttt{reajuste}=0\;\{A_{4}\}\\
&\{A_{4}\}\;\texttt{if …}\;\{A_{5i}\}\\
&\{A_{5i}\}\;\texttt{novoSalario}=salario+salario*percentual\;\{A_{6}\}\\
&\{A_{6}\}\;\texttt{reajuste}=salario*percentual\;\{A_{7}\}\\
&\{A_{7}\}\;\texttt{print(...)}\;\{A_{8}\}\\
&\{A_{8}\}\;\texttt{print(...)}\;\{A_{9}\}\\
&\{A_{9}\}\;\texttt{print(...)}\;\{A_{10}\}=Q .
\end{aligned}
\]

All intermediate triples have been justified by the appropriate Hoare rule,
and the **Consequence** rule was used where necessary to replace the concrete
numeric constants (0.15, 0.12, …) by the abstract expression \(p/100\).

Since the program contains no loops, termination is immediate; therefore the
triple is **totally correct**.

### 4.  Conclusion  

We have exhibited a complete Hoare‑logic derivation that starts from the
pre‑condition  

\[
P\equiv s\in\mathbb R_{2}\land s\ge 0\land\text{input token }s
\]

and ends with the post‑condition  

\[
Q\equiv\exists p\bigl(p=\operatorname{perc}(s)\land
\operatorname{inc}(s)=\tfrac{p}{100}s\land
\operatorname{new}(s)=s+\operatorname{inc}(s)\land
\texttt{out} \text{ as required}\bigr).
\]

Consequently the Python program **conforms to the specification**: for every
admissible salary it prints

* the new salary,
* the amount of the raise,
* and the correct percentage,  

all with the prescribed formatting.  Hence the Hoare triple \(\{P\}\; \texttt{Program}\; \{Q\}\) is proved.   ▢
