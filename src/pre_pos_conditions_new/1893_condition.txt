**Hoare‑style specification**

Let  

* `p₁ , p₂ ∈ ℤ` be the two numbers read from the input  
  (`p₁` – percentage observed two nights ago, `p₂` – percentage observed last night).  

* `out` be the string that the program prints (followed by a line break).

---

### Pre‑condition  

\[
\boxed{P\;:\; 0\le p_{1}\le 100\ \land\ 0\le p_{2}\le 100}
\]

(The program is required to be invoked only with two integer percentages in the
range 0…100.)

---

### Post‑condition  

Define the *phase* function  

\[
\operatorname{phase}(p_{1},p_{2})=
\begin{cases}
\text{``nova''}      &\text{if }0\le p_{2}\le 2,\\[4pt]
\text{``cheia''}     &\text{if }97\le p_{2}\le 100,\\[4pt]
\text{``crescente''}&\text{if }3\le p_{2}\le 96\ \land\ p_{2}>p_{1},\\[4pt]
\text{``minguante''}&\text{if }3\le p_{2}\le 96\ \land\ p_{2}<p_{1}.
\end{cases}
\]

(The specification does not prescribe a result when \(p_{2}=p_{1}\) and
\(3\le p_{2}\le 96\); any of the two increasing/decreasing branches may be
chosen, or an additional clause may be added.)

The post‑condition is then

\[
\boxed{Q\;:\; out = \operatorname{phase}(p_{1},p_{2})\ \land\ \text{``out'' is terminated by a newline}}
\]

---

### Hoare triple for the whole program  

\[
\{\,P\,\}\;\; \texttt{read }p_{1},p_{2};\; \texttt{print(out)}\;\; \{\,Q\,\}
\]

In words: **if** the two input percentages satisfy the pre‑condition (they are
integers between 0 and 100), **then** after the program terminates the printed
string `out` is exactly the moon phase that corresponds to the last‑night
percentage `p₂`, taking into account whether the illumination increased
(`crescente`) or decreased (`minguante`) with respect to the observation two
nights earlier.
