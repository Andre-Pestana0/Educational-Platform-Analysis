**Hoare‑logic specification (pre‑ and post‑conditions only)**  

Let  

* `t` – time spent on the trip (hours) – an integer read from the input,  
* `v` – average speed during the trip (km / h) – an integer read from the input,  
* `c = 12` – the car’s fuel‑efficiency (km per litre),  
* `d` – travelled distance (km),  
* `ℓ` – the amount of fuel (litres) that has to be printed,  

and let `⌊·⌋` denote the floor function.

---

### Pre‑condition  \(P\)

\[
P \;\equiv\; 
\bigl(t\in\mathbb Z \;\wedge\; v\in\mathbb Z\bigr) 
\;\wedge\;
\bigl(t\ge 0 \;\wedge\; v\ge 0\bigr)
\]

The program may assume that the two input values are non‑negative integers (the problem statement guarantees that they are “two integers”).

---

### Post‑condition  \(Q\)

\[
Q \;\equiv\;
\Bigl(
   d = t\cdot v
\Bigr)
\;\wedge\;
\Bigl(
   \ell = 
   \frac{\displaystyle\Bigl\lfloor
          1000\;\frac{d}{c}+ \tfrac12
        \Bigr\rfloor}{1000}
\Bigr)
\;\wedge\;
\bigl(\text{output of the program is the decimal representation of }\ell
      \text{ with exactly three digits after the point}\bigr)
\]

The term  

\[
\frac{\lfloor 1000\frac{d}{c}+ \tfrac12\rfloor}{1000}
\]

is the mathematically precise way to say “round \(\frac{d}{c}\) to three decimal places”.  
Equivalently, one may write  

\[
\ell = \operatorname{round}\!\Bigl(\frac{t\;v}{12},\,3\Bigr)
\]

where \(\operatorname{round}(x,3)\) denotes rounding to the nearest multiple of \(10^{-3}\).

---

### Hoare triple

\[
\boxed{\;\{P\}\; \textbf{read }t; \textbf{read }v;
          \; \ell \gets 
          \frac{\lfloor 1000\frac{t\,v}{12}+ \tfrac12\rfloor}{1000};
          \;\textbf{print } \ell\; \{Q\}\;}
\]

*The program terminates because it consists of a finite sequence of deterministic statements.*  

Thus the formal specification of Little John’s fuel‑calculator is completely captured by the pre‑condition \(P\) (valid input) and the post‑condition \(Q\) (the printed value equals the correctly rounded fuel consumption).
