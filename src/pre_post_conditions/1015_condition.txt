Below is a **Hoare‑logic style specification** for the program that reads the coordinates of two points  
\(p_{1}=(x_{1},y_{1})\) and \(p_{2}=(x_{2},y_{2})\) and prints their Euclidean distance rounded to four
decimal places.

---

### 1.  Notation used  

| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| \(\mathbb{R}\) | set of (finite) real numbers (double‑precision floating‑point values) |
| \(\operatorname{dist}(x_{1},y_{1},x_{2},y_{2})\) | Euclidean distance \(\displaystyle \sqrt{(x_{2}-x_{1})^{2}+(y_{2}-y_{1})^{2}}\) |
| \(\operatorname{round}_{4}(d)\) | rounding of a real number \(d\) to the nearest \(10^{-4}\) (four decimal places)  \(\displaystyle\operatorname{round}_{4}(d)=\frac{\big\lfloor 10^{4}d+0.5\big\rfloor}{10^{4}}\) |
| \(\text{output}\) | the string (or numeric value) that the program writes to *stdout* |
| \(\text{read}(x_{1},y_{1},x_{2},y_{2})\) | the atomic I/O command that consumes the two input lines and stores the four numbers in the
corresponding program variables |

---

### 2.  Hoare triple

\[
\boxed{\; \{P\}\; \texttt{read}(x_{1},y_{1},x_{2},y_{2});\;
      d := \operatorname{dist}(x_{1},y_{1},x_{2},y_{2});\;
      \texttt{print}\bigl(\operatorname{round}_{4}(d)\bigr);\;}
      \{Q\}\;}
\]

---

### 3.  Preconditions  \(P\)

\[
\begin{aligned}
P \; \equiv\; & (x_{1},y_{1},x_{2},y_{2}) \in \mathbb{R}^{4}  \\
              & \;\land\; \text{the input file consists of exactly two lines} \\
              & \;\land\; \text{line 1 contains the two numbers }x_{1}\text{ and }y_{1} \\
              & \;\land\; \text{line 2 contains the two numbers }x_{2}\text{ and }y_{2}.
\end{aligned}
\]

In words: *the program is started with a well‑formed input that supplies four finite
real numbers*.

---

### 4.  Postconditions  \(Q\)

\[
\begin{aligned}
Q \; \equiv\; & d = \operatorname{dist}(x_{1},y_{1},x_{2},y_{2}) \\
              & \;\land\; \text{output} = \operatorname{round}_{4}(d) \\
              & \;\land\; \bigl|\,\text{output} - d\,\bigr| < 5\times10^{-5}.
\end{aligned}
\]

The last inequality is the usual property of rounding to four decimal places:
the printed value differs from the exact distance by less than half of the
\(10^{-4}\) unit.

---

### 5.  Expanded form (optional)

If one prefers to spell out the distance formula explicitly, the postcondition can be written as

\[
\begin{aligned}
Q \equiv\; & d = \sqrt{(x_{2}-x_{1})^{2} + (y_{2}-y_{1})^{2}} \\
           & \land\; \text{output}= \frac{\big\lfloor 10^{4}d+0.5\big\rfloor}{10^{4}} \\
           & \land\; 0 \le \text{output} - d < 10^{-4}.
\end{aligned}
\]

---

### 6.  Summary  

The Hoare triple  

\[
\{P\}\; \text{program}\; \{Q\}
\]

states that **provided the input satisfies the precondition \(P\)** (four real numbers
delivered on two lines), the **program terminates** and **produces as output the
distance between the two points rounded to four decimal places**, as captured by
the postcondition \(Q\). This is the complete formal specification of the
given natural‑language problem.
