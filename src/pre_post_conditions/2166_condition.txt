Below is a **Hoare‑logic style specification** of the program that computes the \(N\)-th convergent of the periodic continued fraction for \(\sqrt{2}\) and prints it with ten decimal digits.

---

### 1.   Auxiliary definitions  

\[
\begin{aligned}
\text{Let }&\operatorname{CF}_0 \;:=\; 1, \\[2mm]
\text{and for }k\ge 1 &
\begin{cases}
x_0   &:= 2,\\[1mm]
x_i   &:= 2+\dfrac{1}{x_{i-1}}\qquad (1\le i\le k),\\[3mm]
\operatorname{CF}_k &:= 1+\dfrac{1}{x_k}.
\end{cases}
\end{aligned}
\]

* \(\operatorname{CF}_k\) is exactly the value of the finite continued fraction  

\[
1+\cfrac{1}{\displaystyle 2+\cfrac{1}{\displaystyle 2+\;\cdots\;+\cfrac{1}{2}}}
\qquad\text{(the denominator “2’’ appears \(k\) times).}
\]

* For \(k=0\) the definition yields \(\operatorname{CF}_0 = 1\).

* The function that the program must return is  

\[
\phi:\{0,\dots,100\}\longrightarrow\mathbb{Q},\qquad
\phi(k)=\operatorname{CF}_k .
\]

* The required printed value is the decimal representation of \(\phi(k)\) **rounded to 10 digits after the decimal point**.  
Denote this rounding operator by  

\[
\operatorname{round}_{10}(r)=\left\lfloor 10^{10}r+0.5\right\rfloor\;10^{-10}.
\]

---

### 2.   Hoare triple  

\[
\boxed{\;\{\,P(N)\,\}\; \texttt{program}\; \{\,Q(N,\text{out})\,\}\;}
\]

where  

\[
\begin{aligned}
P(N) &\;:=\; N\in\mathbb{N}\;\land\;0\le N\le 100, \\[2mm]
Q(N,\text{out}) &\;:=\;
\begin{cases}
\text{out}= \operatorname{round}_{10}\bigl(\phi(N)\bigr) \\
\qquad\land\; |\text{out}-\sqrt{2}|\le 5\cdot10^{-11},
\end{cases}
\end{aligned}
\]

i.e. after termination the variable `out` (the value that is printed) must be the **10‑decimal‑place rounding** of the \(N\)-th convergent \(\phi(N)\).  
The inequality on the right follows from the definition of `round₁₀`; it guarantees that the printed number is the correctly rounded decimal representation.

---

### 3.   Expanded form of the post‑condition (for readability)

\[
\begin{aligned}
Q(N,\text{out}) \equiv\;&
\bigl(N=0\;\Rightarrow\;\text{out}=1.0000000000\bigr) \\
&\land
\bigl(N\ge 1\;\Rightarrow\;
\text{out}= \operatorname{round}_{10}\bigl(1+\frac{1}{x_N}\bigr)\bigr),\\[2mm]
&\text{where }x_0=2,\;x_i=2+\frac{1}{x_{i-1}}\;(1\le i\le N).
\end{aligned}
\]

---

### 4.   Summary in plain words  

* **Pre‑condition**: the input integer `N` satisfies \(0\le N\le 100\).  
* **Post‑condition**: the program prints the number  

\[
\operatorname{round}_{10}\!\bigl(\operatorname{CF}_N\bigr)
\]

where \(\operatorname{CF}_N\) is the \(N\)-fold continued‑fraction approximation of \(\sqrt{2}\) defined above.  

Thus the Hoare triple  

\[
\{0\le N\le 100\}\; \texttt{program}\; \{\texttt{out}= \operatorname{round}_{10}(\operatorname{CF}_N)\}
\]

formally captures the required behaviour of the algorithm.
