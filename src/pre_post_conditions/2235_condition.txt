**Hoare‑logic specification**

Let the program be denoted by `P`.  
It reads three integers `A , B , C` and writes a single character `out` (`'S'` or `'N'`).  

---

### 1.  Pre‑condition  

\[
\boxed{\;{\sf Pre}\;:\;
   A,B,C\in \mathbb N\;\land\;1\le A\le 1000\;\land\;1\le B\le 1000\;\land\;1\le C\le 1000\;}
\]

The three credits are given and each of them lies in the interval \([1,1000]\).

---

### 2.  Post‑condition  

Define three “direction variables”

\[
\varepsilon_A,\;\varepsilon_B,\;\varepsilon_C \in\{-1,0,+1\}
\]

where  

* \(\varepsilon_i = +1\) means “use credit \(i\) to go forward in time”,  
* \(\varepsilon_i = -1\) means “use credit \(i\) to go backward in time”,  
* \(\varepsilon_i = 0\) means “do not use credit \(i\)”.

A *valid travel plan* is a choice of \(\varepsilon_A,\varepsilon_B,\varepsilon_C\) satisfying  

\[
\begin{aligned}
&\text{(i)}\qquad \varepsilon_A A+\varepsilon_B B+\varepsilon_C C = 0 \\[2mm]
&\text{(ii)}\qquad (\varepsilon_A\neq 0)\;\lor\;(\varepsilon_B\neq 0)\;\lor\;(\varepsilon_C\neq 0)
\end{aligned}
\]

Condition (ii) guarantees that at least one trip is performed (the problem allows one, two or three trips; using at most three credits is automatic because we have only three).

The post‑condition of `P` is therefore

\[
\boxed{\;{\sf Post}\;:\;
   out\in\{\text{‘S’},\text{‘N’}\}\;\land\;
   \Bigl(out=\text{‘S’}\;\Longleftrightarrow\;
          \exists\,\varepsilon_A,\varepsilon_B,\varepsilon_C\in\{-1,0,+1\}\;
          \bigl[(\varepsilon_A\neq0\lor\varepsilon_B\neq0\lor\varepsilon_C\neq0)
                \land(\varepsilon_A A+\varepsilon_B B+\varepsilon_C C=0)\bigr]\Bigr)
\;}
\]

In words:

*The program prints ‘S’ **iff** there exists a selection of the three credits, each possibly taken forward (`+1`), backward (`‑1`) or not used (`0`), such that the signed sum of the chosen credits is exactly zero (i.e., we return to the present). Otherwise it prints ‘N’.*

---

### 3.  Hoare triple  

\[
\boxed{\;\{\,{\sf Pre}\,\}\;P\; \{\,{\sf Post}\,\}\;}
\]

This triple completely captures the required behaviour of the algorithm: any execution that starts with three admissible credits must terminate with the character ‘S’ precisely when a feasible combination of forward/backward trips exists, and with ‘N’ otherwise.
