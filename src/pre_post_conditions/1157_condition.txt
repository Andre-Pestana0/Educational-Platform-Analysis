Below is a **Hoare‑style specification** for the “read an integer \(N\) and print all its positive divisors” task.  
Only the *pre‑condition* (what must hold before the program starts) and the *post‑condition* (what must hold after it finishes) are given; the body of the program is omitted.

---

### Notation used  

* \(\mathbb Z\) – set of all integers.  
* \(\mathbb N^+ = \{1,2,3,\dots\}\) – set of **positive** integers.  
* \(\operatorname{mod}(a,b)\) – the remainder of the Euclidean division of \(a\) by \(b\).  
* \(\,|\,\) – cardinality of a finite set.  
* \(\operatorname{Div}(N) \;=\;\{d\in\mathbb N^+ \mid \operatorname{mod}(N,d)=0\}\) – the set of all positive divisors of \(N\).  
* \(\operatorname{out}\) – the sequence of numbers that the program writes to the output file, one per line; we write \(\operatorname{out}[i]\) for its \(i\)-th element (1‑based indexing).  
* \(\operatorname{len}(\operatorname{out})\) – length of that sequence.  

---

## Hoare triple  

\[
\boxed{\{\,P(N)\,\}\; \texttt{Program}\; \{\,Q(N,\operatorname{out})\,\}}
\]

### Pre‑condition  \(P(N)\)

\[
\begin{aligned}
P(N) \; \equiv\; & N \in \mathbb Z \;\land\; N > 0               \\
                & \;\land\; \text{“the input file contains exactly the integer }N\text{”.}
\end{aligned}
\]

*(The problem statement says “read an integer \(N\)”.  We require the integer to be
strictly positive so that the notion of *positive* divisors is well‑defined.)*

---

### Post‑condition  \(Q(N,\operatorname{out})\)

\[
\begin{aligned}
Q(N,\operatorname{out}) \; \equiv\; & \operatorname{len}(\operatorname{out}) = |\operatorname{Div}(N)| \\[2mm]
 & \land\; \bigl(\forall i\in\{1,\dots,\operatorname{len}(\operatorname{out})\}\;:
        \operatorname{out}[i]\in\mathbb N^+ \land \operatorname{mod}(N,\operatorname{out}[i]) = 0\bigr) \\[2mm]
 & \land\; \bigl(\forall i\neq j\;:\; \operatorname{out}[i]\neq\operatorname{out}[j]\bigr)      \qquad\text{(no repetitions)}\\[2mm]
 & \land\; \bigl(\forall d\in\operatorname{Div}(N)\;:\; \exists i\;:\; \operatorname{out}[i]=d\bigr) 
        \qquad\text{(completeness)}\\[2mm]
 & \land\; \bigl(\forall i,j\;:\; 1\le i<j\le\operatorname{len}(\operatorname{out}) \;\Rightarrow\;
        \operatorname{out}[i] < \operatorname{out}[j]\bigr) 
        \qquad\text{(strictly increasing order)}\\[2mm]
 & \land\; \text{the program terminates (halts).}
\end{aligned}
\]

In words, after termination the program has produced a *finite sequence* whose

* length equals the number of positive divisors of \(N\);
* every element is a positive divisor of \(N\);
* each positive divisor of \(N\) appears **exactly once**;
* the elements are listed in strictly increasing order (the example output follows this order, though the order requirement can be omitted if desired).

---

### Full specification

\[
\boxed{
\begin{array}{l}
\{\, N\in\mathbb Z \land N>0 \,\}\\[1mm]
\texttt{read }N; \; \texttt{compute }L; \; \texttt{write each }L_i\text{ on a new line}\\[1mm]
\{\, \operatorname{len}(L)=|\operatorname{Div}(N)|
   \;\land\; \forall i\!:\!L_i\in\mathbb N^+ \land \operatorname{mod}(N,L_i)=0\\
\qquad\land\; \forall i\neq j\!:\!L_i\neq L_j
   \;\land\; \forall d\in\operatorname{Div}(N)\!:\!\exists i\!:\!L_i=d\\
\qquad\land\; \forall i<j\!:\!L_i<L_j \,\}
\end{array}
}
\]

That is the complete Hoare‑logic style **pre‑/post‑condition specification** for the given natural‑language problem.
