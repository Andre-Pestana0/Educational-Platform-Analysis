**Hoare‑style specification**

We denote  

* `x , y ∈ ℤ` – the two integer values read from the input,  
* `s ∈ ℤ` – the integer that the program prints,  
* `MAX_INT` – the greatest integer that can be represented in the target language,  
* `odd(i) ≡ (i  \bmod 2 ≠ 0)` – the predicate “*i* is odd”,  

and we introduce the auxiliary bounds  

\[
\begin{aligned}
a &:= \min (x,y)+1,\\[2pt]
b &:= \max (x,y)-1 .
\end{aligned}
\]

If the interval is empty (`a>b`) the sum is defined to be 0.

---

### Pre‑condition  

\[
\boxed{P \;:\; x\in\mathbb Z \;\wedge\; y\in\mathbb Z \;\wedge\;
|x|\le MAX\_INT \;\wedge\; |y|\le MAX\_INT}
\]

(Only the requirement that the two inputs are representable integers is needed.)

---

### Post‑condition  

\[
\boxed{
\begin{aligned}
Q \;:\;& s = \sum_{i=a}^{\,b}\; odd(i)\cdot i \\
      &\qquad\text{(the sum is 0 if }a>b\text{)}\\[4pt]
      &\wedge\; s\in\mathbb Z \;\wedge\; |s|\le MAX\_INT
\end{aligned}}
\]

In words: `s` equals the sum of every odd integer strictly between the two
input values (order‑independent), and the result also fits in an integer.
