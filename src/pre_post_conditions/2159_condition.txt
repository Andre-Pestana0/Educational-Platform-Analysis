**Hoare‑style specification**

\[
\{\,\text{Pre}\,\}\; \texttt{ComputeBounds}\; \{\,\text{Post}\,\}
\]

---

### 1.  Pre‑condition  

\[
\text{Pre}\;:\;
\begin{aligned}
& n \in \mathbb{N} \;\land\; 17 \le n \le 10^{9}                \tag{1}\\[2mm]
&\pi(n)=\bigl|\{p\in\mathbb{N}\mid p\text{ is prime }\land p\le n\}\bigr| \tag{2}\\[2mm]
&\ln :\mathbb{R}_{>0}\rightarrow\mathbb{R} \text{ is the natural logarithm.}
\end{aligned}
\]

---

### 2.  Auxiliary definitions  

* **Lower bound function**  

  \[
  L(n)=\frac{n}{\ln n}
  \]

* **Upper bound function** (Rosser–Schoenfeld constant)  

  \[
  \alpha = 1.25506,\qquad 
  U(n)=\alpha\;\frac{n}{\ln n}
  \]

* **Rounding to one decimal place**  

  \[
  \operatorname{round}_{1}(x)=\frac{\bigl\lfloor 10x+0.5 \bigr\rfloor}{10}
  \qquad\bigl(\text{the nearest tenth of }x\bigr)
  \]

---

### 3.  Post‑condition  

Let  

\[
P = \operatorname{round}_{1}\bigl(L(n)\bigr),\qquad
M = \operatorname{round}_{1}\bigl(U(n)\bigr).
\]

Then the program must output the ordered pair \((P,M)\) and the following
property must hold:

\[
\boxed{\,P < \pi(n) < M\,}                                            \tag{3}
\]

together with the typing constraints

\[
P,M \in \mathbb{Q},\qquad \text{each has exactly one digit after the decimal point.}
\]

---

### 4.  Full Hoare triple  

\[
\boxed{
\begin{array}{l}
\{\, n\in\mathbb{N}\land 17\le n\le 10^{9}\,\}\\[1mm]
\texttt{ComputeBounds}\\[1mm]
\{\, P=\operatorname{round}_{1}\!\bigl(\tfrac{n}{\ln n}\bigr)\;\land\;
   M=\operatorname{round}_{1}\!\bigl(1.25506\;\tfrac{n}{\ln n}\bigr)\;\land\\
\qquad\qquad P<M\;\land\;P<\pi(n)<M\;\land\;
   P,M\text{ have one decimal digit}\,\}
\end{array}}
\]
