**Hoare‑logic specification**

Let  

* `out` be the (finite) list of strings that the program writes to the standard output,  
* `I` and `J` be the loop variables that appear in the printed lines,  

and define the two finite sets  

\[
\mathcal I = \{\,1,3,5,7,9\,\},\qquad 
\mathcal J = \{\,7,6,5\,\}.
\]

We also define the *lexicographic* ordering on the Cartesian product  
\(\mathcal I\times\mathcal J\) that is induced by the **outer** loop on `I` and the **inner** loop on `J` :

\[
\langle(i_0,j_0),\dots,(i_{14},j_{14})\rangle
   =\bigl\langle (1,7),(1,6),(1,5),
                (3,7),(3,6),(3,5),
                (5,7),(5,6),(5,5),
                (7,7),(7,6),(7,5),
                (9,7),(9,6),(9,5)\bigr\rangle .
\]

For a pair \((i,j)\) we write the corresponding output line as  

\[
\operatorname{line}(i,j)=\text{``I="}\;||\;i\;||\;\text{`` J="}\;||\;j\;||\;\text{``\textbackslash n''}.
\]

---

### Pre‑condition  

Because the problem has **no input**, the only requirement before the program starts is that the
output buffer is empty (or, equivalently, that we make no assumptions about the environment).

\[
\boxed{P\;:\; \text{true} \;\;\;(\text{or } out = \langle\rangle)}
\]

---

### Post‑condition  

After termination the output buffer must contain **exactly** the 15 lines listed above, in the
order given by the lexicographic product \(\langle(i_k,j_k)\rangle_{k=0}^{14}\).

\[
\boxed{Q\;:\;
\begin{aligned}
out &= \big\langle\operatorname{line}(i_0,j_0),\,
               \operatorname{line}(i_1,j_1),\dots,
               \operatorname{line}(i_{14},j_{14})\big\rangle \\[2mm]
    &\text{where } \langle(i_k,j_k)\rangle_{k=0}^{14}
      =\big\langle (i,j)\mid i\in\mathcal I,\;j\in\mathcal J\big\rangle_{\text{lex}} .
\end{aligned}}
\]

Equivalently, using explicit quantification:

\[
\begin{aligned}
Q \equiv\;&\forall k\in\{0,\dots,14\}\;
          \bigl(out[k]=\operatorname{line}(i_k,j_k)\bigr)\\
        &\land\;|out|=15\\
        &\land\;\bigl(i_k,j_k\bigr)=
          \begin{cases}
            (1,7) & k=0\\
            (1,6) & k=1\\
            (1,5) & k=2\\
            (3,7) & k=3\\
            (3,6) & k=4\\
            (3,5) & k=5\\
            \;\vdots\\
            (9,5) & k=14
          \end{cases}
\end{aligned}
\]

---

### Hoare triple  

\[
\boxed{\{\,P\,\}\;\; \text{program}\;\; \{\,Q\,\}}
\]

where the *program* is the straightforward double‑loop implementation:

```pseudo
for I ← 1 to 9 step 2 do
    for J ← 7 downto 5 do
        print "I=" , I , " J=" , J
```

The Hoare triple asserts that, starting from any state (no input required), after the program
terminates the only observable effect is the exact sequence of lines described in `Q`.
