Below is a **Hoare‑logic style specification** for the program that produces exactly the eight lines shown in the statement.  
Only the *pre‑condition* (what is known before the program starts) and the *post‑condition* (what must hold after the program terminates) are given – no implementation details are required.

---

### 1. Notation used

* `⊤` – the always‑true proposition (used as a trivial pre‑condition).  
* `⟨…⟩` – the (finite) sequence of characters (a string).  
* `&` – concatenation of strings.  
* `pad(k)` – the string consisting of `k` space characters (`' '`), i.e.  

  \[
  \operatorname{pad}(k)=\underbrace{\text{`` ``}\dots\text{`` ``}}_{k\ \text{times}}
  \]

* `\newline` – the line‑break character that separates output lines.  
* `out` – the whole output produced by the program (a single string that contains the eight lines separated by `\newline`).  

We also introduce two constant phrases that appear in the output

\[
\begin{aligned}
S &:= \text{``AMO FAZER EXERCICIO NO URI''}\\[2mm]
T &:= \text{``AMO FAZER EXERCICIO''}
\end{aligned}
\]

---

### 2. Definition of the eight output lines

\[
\begin{aligned}
\ell_1 &:= \text{``<''} &\; &\!\!& S &\;\text{``>''} \\[1mm]
\ell_2 &:= \text{``<''} &\; &\!\!& \operatorname{pad}(4) &\; S &\;\text{``>''} \\[1mm]
\ell_3 &:= \text{``<''} &\; &\!\!& T &\; \operatorname{pad}(1) &\;\text{``>''} \\[1mm]
\ell_4 &:= \ell_1 \\[1mm]
\ell_5 &:= \text{``<''} &\; &\!\!& S &\; \operatorname{pad}(4) &\;\text{``>''} \\[1mm]
\ell_6 &:= \ell_1 \\[1mm]
\ell_7 &:= \text{``<''} &\; &\!\!& \operatorname{pad}(10) &\; T &\; \operatorname{pad}(1) &\;\text{``>''} \\[1mm]
\ell_8 &:= \text{``<''} &\; &\!\!& T &\; \operatorname{pad}(11) &\;\text{``>''}
\end{aligned}
\]

(Every `\text{``<''}` and `\text{``>''}` is a single angle‑bracket character.)

---

### 3. Post‑condition (what the program must output)

The required final output is the **exact concatenation** of the eight lines, each terminated by a line‑break (except the last one, which may or may not be followed by a final newline – both conventions are accepted by most judges). Formally

\[
\boxed{
\begin{aligned}
\mathsf{post}\;(out) \;\equiv\; & out \;=\; \ell_1\;\newline\;
                                 \ell_2\;\newline\;
                                 \ell_3\;\newline\;
                                 \ell_4\;\newline\;
                                 \ell_5\;\newline\;
                                 \ell_6\;\newline\;
                                 \ell_7\;\newline\;
                                 \ell_8
\end{aligned}}
\]

---

### 4. Hoare triple for the whole program

Because the problem statement says **“Input: there is none”**, the only thing we know before execution is that no external data is read. Hence the pre‑condition is simply *true* (or `⊤`).

\[
\boxed{\;\; \{\,\top\,\}\;\; \textbf{Program}\;\; \{\,\mathsf{post}(out)\,\}\;\;}
\]

---

### 5. Summary (in plain English)

* **Pre‑condition**: there are no inputs; the program starts in an arbitrary state (`⊤`).  
* **Post‑condition**: after termination, the program’s output `out` consists of the eight lines listed above, each line exactly as shown (including every space and the surrounding angle brackets).

That specification, expressed in the usual Hoare‑logic notation, captures the whole requirement of the “no‑input / fixed‑output” exercise.
