**Hoare‑style specification**

We describe the program that receives the six integer inputs  

\[
H,E,A,O,W,X\in\mathbb Z
\]

(the numbers of humans, elves, dwarves, orcs, wargs and eagles) and prints one of the two
messages

* `Middle‑earth is safe.`  
* `Sauron has returned.`  

The specification is given only by a **pre‑condition** \(P\) and a **post‑condition** \(Q\).

---

### Pre‑condition  \(P\)

\[
\boxed{
P \;\equiv\; 
\bigl(H\ge 0\;\wedge\;E\ge 0\;\wedge\;A\ge 0\;\wedge\;O\ge 0\;\wedge\;W\ge 0\;\wedge\;X\ge 0\bigr)
}
\]

All six inputs are non‑negative integers (the natural numbers \(\mathbb N\)).  
No other assumptions are made.

---

### Auxiliary definitions (used in the post‑condition)

\[
\begin{aligned}
G &:= H + E + A          &&\text{(initial “good” army)}\\[2mm]
B &:= O + W              &&\text{(initial “evil” army)}\\[2mm]
\Delta &:= 
\begin{cases}
X & \text{if } G \le B \\[1mm]
0 & \text{if } G > B
\end{cases}
&&\text{(size of the “eagle” reinforcement)}\\[2mm]
G' &:= G + \Delta        &&\text{(final good army after possible reinforcement)}
\end{aligned}
\]

---

### Post‑condition  \(Q\)

Let `out` be the string printed by the program.  
The post‑condition states precisely which string must be produced:

\[
\boxed{
\begin{aligned}
Q \;\equiv\; &\bigl(out = \text{``Middle‑earth is safe.''}\bigr) 
               \;\;\Longleftrightarrow\;\;
               G' \;\ge\; B \\[1mm]
             &\;\;\wedge\;
               \bigl(out = \text{``Sauron has returned.''}\bigr) 
               \;\;\Longleftrightarrow\;\;
               G' \;<\; B
\end{aligned}
}
\]

In words:

* If the **final good army** (the original good army plus the eagles, but only when the
  original good army does **not** already dominate the evil army) is **greater than or
  equal to** the evil army, the program must output  

  `Middle‑earth is safe.`

* Otherwise (the final good army is strictly smaller) the program must output  

  `Sauron has returned.`

---

### Hoare triple

Putting the pieces together, the whole program is specified by the Hoare triple  

\[
\boxed{\{P\}\; \texttt{BattleOfFiveArmies}\; \{Q\}}
\]

where `BattleOfFiveArmies` denotes any algorithm that:

1. reads the six non‑negative integers \(H,E,A,O,W,X\);
2. computes the quantities \(G,B,\Delta,G'\) as defined above;
3. prints `Middle‑earth is safe.` iff \(G' \ge B\), otherwise prints `Sauron has returned.`

The triple guarantees that **every** execution that starts in a state satisfying the
pre‑condition will terminate with an output satisfying the post‑condition. ∎
