Below is a **Hoare‑logic style specification** of the problem.  
Only the *pre‑condition* (what the caller must guarantee) and the *post‑condition* (what the program must guarantee on termination) are given – the body of the program is omitted, as requested.

---

### 1.  Vocabulary  

* Let  

\[
\mathbb Y = \{2011,\dots ,2011+n-1\}
\]

be the set of years for which information is supplied.  

* A **moose** is a pair  

\[
m = (e(m),s(m))\in\mathbb Y\times\mathbb N,
\]

where  

* \(e(m)\)  – year of entry into the tournament (the *entry year*),  
* \(s(m)\) – strength (a non‑negative integer, all strengths are pairwise distinct).

* The whole input is a finite set  

\[
M = \{m_1,\dots ,m_{k+n-1}\}
\]

with the following distinguished element  

\[
m_K = (y,p) \in M
\]

(the *Karl‑Älgtav* moose).

* For a year \(t\in\mathbb Y\) let  

\[
P_t = \bigl\{\, m\in M \mid e(m)\le t \text{ and } m\notin W_{<t}\,\bigr\}
\]

be the **pool** of contestants that are still present at the beginning of year \(t\).  

* \(W_{<t}\) denotes the set of winners of all earlier years:  

\[
W_{<t}= \{\, w_{t'}\mid t'\in\mathbb Y,\;t'<t \,\}.
\]

* The **winner** of year \(t\) is the (unique) moose with maximal strength in the pool:

\[
w_t = \operatorname*{arg\,max}_{m\in P_t} s(m).
\]

Because strengths are unique, \(w_t\) is well defined.

* The **output** of the program is a value  

\[
\mathit{out}\in\mathbb Y\cup\{\text{“unknown”}\}.
\]

---

### 2.  Pre‑condition  

\[
\begin{aligned}
\{\,\mathit{Pre}\,\}\; \equiv\;&
\bigl(k\in[1,10^{5}]\bigr)\;\land\;
\bigl(n\in[1,10^{5}]\bigr)                                            \\[2mm]
&\land\;
\bigl(y\in[2011,\,2011+n-1]\bigr)\;\land\;
\bigl(p\in[0,2^{31}-1]\bigr)                                         \\[2mm]
&\land\;
\bigl(m_K=(y,p)\in M\bigr)                                            \\[2mm]
&\land\;
\bigl|\,\{\,m\in M\mid e(m)=2011\,\}\bigr| = k                         \\[2mm]
&\land\;
\bigl|\,\{\,m\in M\mid e(m)\neq 2011\,\}\bigr| = n-1                \\[2mm]
&\land\;
\bigl(\forall m,m'\in M\;(m\neq m' \;\Rightarrow\; s(m)\neq s(m'))\bigr)\\[2mm]
&\land\;
\bigl(\forall m,m'\in M\;(e(m)=e(m')\neq 2011 \;\Rightarrow\; \bot)\bigr)
\qquad\text{(the non‑2011 entry years are all distinct)}.
\end{aligned}
\]

In words:  

* the numbers \(k,n\) satisfy the limits of the statement,  
* Karl’s entry year and strength are within the allowed ranges,  
* the input set \(M\) contains exactly \(k+n-1\) moose,  
* exactly \(k\) of them (including possibly Karl) entered in 2011,  
* the remaining \(n-1\) have pairwise different entry years,  
* all strengths are distinct.

---

### 3.  Post‑condition  

Let  

\[
T = \{\, t\in\mathbb Y \mid w_t = m_K \,\}
\]

be the set of years in which Karl would be the winner **according to the data that are known** (i.e. assuming that no further moose will ever enter after year \(2011+n-1\)).  

Define  

\[
t^{*}= \min T\quad\text{if }T\neq\varnothing .
\]

The program’s result \(\mathit{out}\) must satisfy

\[
\begin{aligned}
\{\,\mathit{Post}\,\}\; \equiv\;&
\bigl(\mathit{out}\in\mathbb Y \;\land\; \mathit{out}=t^{*}\bigr)                           \\[2mm]
&\;\lor\; \bigl(\mathit{out}=\text{“unknown”}\;\land\;
      \bigl(\forall\,\tilde M\supseteq M\;(\tilde M\text{ respects the pre‑condition})\;
            \exists\,t\in\mathbb Y\; \bigl(w_t^{\tilde M}=m_K\bigr)\; \Rightarrow\;
            t\neq t^{*}\bigr)\bigr) .
\end{aligned}
\]

Interpretation:

* **Case 1 (a concrete year is returned).**  
  If the algorithm outputs a year \(\mathit{out}=t\), then \(t\) is the *earliest* year in the supplied interval \(\mathbb Y\) in which Karl is the winner, and **any** possible extension of the data (adding further moose for later years) that respects the pre‑condition would **not** change this answer – Karl would still win exactly in year \(t\).

* **Case 2 (“unknown” is returned).**  
  The algorithm may output “unknown” only when the supplied information is insufficient to guarantee a unique winning year. Formally, there exists at least one admissible completion \(\tilde M\) of the input (adding any number of future moose with entry years \(>2011+n-1\) and strengths distinct from all already given) such that Karl wins in some year \(t_1\) and another admissible completion \(\tilde M'\) in which Karl wins in a *different* year \(t_2\) (or never wins). Hence the winning year cannot be uniquely determined from the given data.

---

### 4.  Whole Hoare Triple  

\[
\boxed{\;\{\,\mathit{Pre}\,\}\;\; \texttt{solve\_alpha\_moose}(k,n,M)\;\; \{\,\mathit{Post}\,\}\;}
\]

where `solve_alpha_moose` denotes the (unspecified) program that reads the input described by the pre‑condition and produces the output described by the post‑condition.  

The specification captures **exactly** the required behaviour of the problem:  

* it respects all size and uniqueness constraints,  
* it models the yearly tournament by the maximal‑strength rule,  
* it returns the unique year in which Karl becomes the alpha‑moose when that year is forced by the supplied information, and otherwise returns the string “unknown”.
