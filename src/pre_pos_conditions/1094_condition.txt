Below is a **Hoare‑logic style specification** for a program that reads the data described in the statement, computes the required totals and percentages, and prints them.  
Only the *pre‑condition* (what must hold before the program starts) and the *post‑condition* (what must hold after it terminates) are given – the body of the program is omitted on purpose, because the request was for the formal specification only.

---

### 1. Notation

| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| `ℕ`    | set of natural numbers (including 0) |
| `ℤ⁺`   | set of positive integers |
| `ℝ`    | set of real numbers |
| `⟦·⟧`  | “the value of” (semantic brackets) |
| `Σ_{i=1}^{k} e_i` | sum of the expressions `e_i` for `i = 1 … k` |
| `type_i ∈ {C,R,S}` | the animal type of the *i*‑th record |
| `amount_i ∈ ℤ⁺` | the number of animals of that record |
| `⌊x⌋` | floor (integer part) of a real `x` |
| `round₂(x)` | rounding of the real `x` to **exactly two decimal digits** (ties to even, as required by the usual “printf %0.2f” semantics) |
| `output = ( … )` | the concrete string that the program prints (see the “output format” clause below) |

All indices start at 1.

---

### 2. Variables used in the specification  

| Variable | Type | Intended meaning |
|----------|------|-------------------|
| `N`      | `ℤ⁺` | number of test cases (records) |
| `amount_i` | `ℤ⁺` (1 ≤ `amount_i` ≤ 15) | amount of animals in the *i*‑th record |
| `type_i` | `{C,R,S}` | type of animal in the *i*‑th record |
| `total`  | `ℤ⁺` | total number of animals over all records |
| `total_C`| `ℤ⁺` | total number of **C** (coelhos) |
| `total_R`| `ℤ⁺` | total number of **R** (ratos) |
| `total_S`| `ℤ⁺` | total number of **S** (sapos) |
| `perc_C` | `ℝ` (0 ≤ `perc_C` ≤ 100) | percentage of coelhos w.r.t. `total` |
| `perc_R` | `ℝ` (0 ≤ `perc_R` ≤ 100) | percentage of ratos w.r.t. `total` |
| `perc_S` | `ℝ` (0 ≤ `perc_S` ≤ 100) | percentage of sapos w.r.t. `total` |
| `out`    | string | the exact text printed by the program |

---

### 3. Pre‑condition  **P**

\[
\begin{aligned}
P \; \equiv\; & N \in \mathbb{Z}^{+} \\
            & \land\; \forall i \in \{1,\dots ,N\}\;.\; 1 \leq \text{amount}_i \leq 15 \\
            & \land\; \forall i \in \{1,\dots ,N\}\;.\; \text{type}_i \in \{C,R,S\}
\end{aligned}
\]

In words: the program receives a positive integer `N`, followed by `N` pairs
`(amount_i , type_i)` such that every amount lies in the interval \([1,15]\) and
every type is one of the three admissible characters.

---

### 4. Post‑condition  **Q**

\[
\begin{aligned}
Q \; \equiv\; & \text{total} \;=\; \sum_{i=1}^{N} \text{amount}_i \\[4pt]
            & \land\; \text{total\_C} \;=\; \sum_{i=1}^{N} \bigl[\text{type}_i = C\bigr] \cdot \text{amount}_i \\[4pt]
            & \land\; \text{total\_R} \;=\; \sum_{i=1}^{N} \bigl[\text{type}_i = R\bigr] \cdot \text{amount}_i \\[4pt]
            & \land\; \text{total\_S} \;=\; \sum_{i=1}^{N} \bigl[\text{type}_i = S\bigr] \cdot \text{amount}_i \\[4pt]
            & \land\; \text{total} \;=\; \text{total\_C} + \text{total\_R} + \text{total\_S} \\[6pt]
            & \land\; \text{perc\_C} \;=\; round_{2}\!\Bigl(100 \cdot \frac{\text{total\_C}}{\text{total}}\Bigr) \\[4pt]
            & \land\; \text{perc\_R} \;=\; round_{2}\!\Bigl(100 \cdot \frac{\text{total\_R}}{\text{total}}\Bigr) \\[4pt]
            & \land\; \text{perc\_S} \;=\; round_{2}\!\Bigl(100 \cdot \frac{\text{total\_S}}{\text{total}}\Bigr) \\[6pt]
            & \land\; \text{out} \;=\; \\
            & \quad\text{“Total: ”} \; \Vert\; \text{total} \; \Vert\; \text{“ cobaias\n”} \\
            & \quad\Vert\; \text{“Total de coelhos: ”} \; \Vert\; \text{total\_C} \; \Vert\; \text{“\n”} \\
            & \quad\Vert\; \text{“Total de ratos: ”} \; \Vert\; \text{total\_R} \; \Vert\; \text{“\n”} \\
            & \quad\Vert\; \text{“Total de sapos: ”} \; \Vert\; \text{total\_S} \; \Vert\; \text{“\n”} \\
            & \quad\Vert\; \text{“Percentual de coelhos: ”} \; \Vert\; \text{perc\_C} \; \Vert\; \text{“ %\n”} \\
            & \quad\Vert\; \text{“Percentual de ratos: ”} \; \Vert\; \text{perc\_R} \; \Vert\; \text{“ %\n”} \\
            & \quad\Vert\; \text{“Percentual de sapos: ”} \; \Vert\; \text{perc\_S} \; \Vert\; \text{“ %\n”}
\end{aligned}
\]

Explanation of the clauses:

* `[…]` is the **Iverson bracket**: it evaluates to `1` if the condition holds
  and `0` otherwise.  
  Hence, for example `Σ_{i=1}^{N} [type_i = C]·amount_i` adds `amount_i` only
  when the record’s type is `C`.

* `round₂(x)` denotes **round‑to‑nearest with two decimal digits** (the same
  effect as printing `x` with the format specifier `"%0.2f"`).  

* The string concatenation operator `‖` builds the exact output that must be
  produced.  New‑line characters are written as `\n`.  The percentages are
  followed by a space and the character `%` exactly as shown in the sample
  output.

* The equality `total = total_C + total_R + total_S` is a derived property that
  follows from the definitions of the three subtotals; it is included in the
  post‑condition to make the relationship explicit.

---

### 5. Hoare triple

Putting the two parts together, the formal specification of the whole program
is the Hoare triple

\[
\boxed{\{\,P\,\}\;\; \textbf{Program}\;\; \{\,Q\,\}}
\]

where  

*`P`* is the pre‑condition described in section 3, and  
*`Q`* is the post‑condition described in section 4.

Any implementation that, when started in a state satisfying `P`, terminates
with a state satisfying `Q` (and does not diverge) is **correct** with respect
to the problem
