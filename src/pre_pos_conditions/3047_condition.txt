Below is a **Hoare‑logic style specification** of the whole program (the “algorithm”) that solves the problem.  
Only the **pre‑condition** (what must be true before the program starts) and the **post‑condition** (what is guaranteed to be true when the program finishes) are given; the actual statements of the program are omitted because they are obvious (compute the missing child’s age and then take the maximum).

---

### Variables  

| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|----------|
| `M`    | Mrs. Monica’s age (input) |
| `A`    | Age of the first known child (input) |
| `B`    | Age of the second known child (input) |
| `C`    | Age of the third child (computed inside the program) |
| `O`    | Age of the oldest child (output) |

All variables range over the set of non‑negative integers **ℕ** (in practice strictly positive ages).

---

### Helper notation  

* `max(x₁,…,x_k)` – the greatest of the arguments.  
* `≠` – inequality.  
* `∧` – logical conjunction.  
* `∃` – “there exists”.  

---

## Hoare Triple  

\[
\boxed{\{\,P\,\}\; \text{program}\; \{\,Q\,\}}
\]

where  

### Pre‑condition \(P\)

\[
\begin{aligned}
P \;\equiv\; &
\bigl(M\in\mathbb N \;\wedge\; A\in\mathbb N \;\wedge\; B\in\mathbb N\bigr)                         \\[2mm]
&\;\wedge\; \bigl(M > A \;\wedge\; M > B\bigr)                                                \\[2mm]
&\;\wedge\; \bigl(A \neq B\bigr)                                                            \\[2mm]
&\;\wedge\; \bigl(\exists C\in\mathbb N\;.\; C = M - A - B \;\wedge\; C>0 \;\wedge\; C\neq A
                \;\wedge\; C\neq B\bigr)                                                    \\[2mm]
&\;\wedge\; \bigl(M = A + B + C\bigr)            \qquad\text{(the “sum‑equals‑mother” condition)} 
\end{aligned}
\]

*Interpretation*: the three input ages are non‑negative integers, each child is younger than the mother, the two known children have different ages, there exists a third child’s age `C` that makes the sum of the three children’s ages exactly `M`, and all three children have pairwise distinct ages (as required by the statement “three children of different ages”).

---

### Post‑condition \(Q\)

\[
\begin{aligned}
Q \;\equiv\; &
\bigl(O \in \mathbb N\bigr)                                          \\[1mm]
&\;\wedge\; \bigl(O = \max\{A,\,B,\,C\}\bigr)                        \\[1mm]
&\;\wedge\; \bigl(O = \text{age of the oldest child}\bigr)
\end{aligned}
\]

*Interpretation*: after termination the program returns a non‑negative integer `O` that is exactly the greatest of the three children’s ages, i.e. the age of the oldest child.

---

## Sketch of the Program (for completeness)

```
C := M - A - B;               // compute the missing child’s age
O := max(A, B, C);            // pick the oldest
output O;
```

The Hoare triple above proves that, **provided the pre‑condition holds**, the program terminates and the output satisfies the post‑condition – which is precisely the requirement of the problem statement.
