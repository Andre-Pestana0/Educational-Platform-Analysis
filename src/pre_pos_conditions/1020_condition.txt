**Hoare‑style specification**

Let  

* `n` be the integer read from the input (age expressed in days).  
* `⌊x⌋` denote the integer floor of a rational number `x`.  
* `a mod b` denote the remainder of the Euclidean division of `a` by `b` (so `0 ≤ a mod b < b`).  

Define the auxiliary quantities  

\[
\begin{aligned}
y &:= \Big\lfloor \frac{n}{365} \Big\rfloor ,                         &&\text{(whole years)}\\[2mm]
r &:= n \bmod 365 ,                                                &&\text{(days left after removing full years)}\\[2mm]
m &:= \Big\lfloor \frac{r}{30} \Big\rfloor ,                        &&\text{(whole months)}\\[2mm]
d &:= r \bmod 30 .                                                  &&\text{(remaining days)}
\end{aligned}
\]

---

### Pre‑condition  \(P\)

\[
P \;\equiv\; n \in \mathbb Z \;\land\; n \ge 0
\]

(The program receives exactly one non‑negative integer representing a number of days.)

---

### Post‑condition  \(Q\)

\[
\begin{aligned}
Q \;\equiv\;&\; \text{The program prints three lines exactly in the order}\\
            &\quad\text{“}y\ \text{ano(s)"}\\
            &\quad\text{“}m\ \text{mes(es)"}\\
            &\quad\text{“}d\ \text{dia(s)"}\\[2mm]
          &\;\land\; y = \Big\lfloor \frac{n}{365} \Big\rfloor\\
          &\;\land\; r = n \bmod 365\\
          &\;\land\; m = \Big\lfloor \frac{r}{30} \Big\rfloor\\
          &\;\land\; d = r \bmod 30 .
\end{aligned}
\]

---

### Hoare triple

\[
\boxed{\{\,P\,\}\; \text{ReadAgeAndPrint}\; \{\,Q\,\}}
\]

where `ReadAgeAndPrint` is the (imperative) program that

1. reads the integer `n` from standard input,
2. computes the values `y`, `m`, `d` as defined above,
3. prints the three lines `y ano(s)`, `m mes(es)`, `d dia(s)`.

The triple states that **if** the input satisfies the pre‑condition (a single non‑negative integer), **then** after termination the output satisfies the post‑condition (the correct decomposition into years, months and days, with the required textual format).
