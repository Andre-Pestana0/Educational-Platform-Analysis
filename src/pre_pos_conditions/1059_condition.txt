**Hoare‑logic specification**

Let  

* `out` be the (finite) sequence that the program writes to the standard output,  
* `len(out)` be its length,  
* `out[i]` (for `1 ≤ i ≤ len(out)`) be the *i*‑th line (interpreted as an integer),  

and define  

\[
\operatorname{Even}(n)\;\;:\;\; n\;\bmod 2 = 0 .
\]

---

### Pre‑condition  

There is no input, therefore the only requirement before the program starts is that the output buffer is empty:

\[
\boxed{P\; \equiv\; \text{true}\qquad(\text{or } out = \langle\rangle )}
\]

---

### Post‑condition  

After termination the program must have printed **exactly** the even integers from 1 to 100, one per line, in increasing order.  This can be expressed in two equivalent ways.

1. **Explicit sequence form**

\[
\boxed{Q\; \equiv\; 
\begin{aligned}
&\; len(out)=50,\\[2pt]
&\; \forall k\in\{1,\dots,50\}\;:\; out[k]=2k .
\end{aligned}}
\]

Thus  

\[
out = \langle 2,4,6,\dots ,100\rangle .
\]

2. **Set‑and‑order form**

\[
\boxed{Q\; \equiv\;
\begin{aligned}
&\; \{\, out[i]\mid 1\le i\le len(out)\,\}= \{\, n\in\mathbb N \mid 1\le n\le 100\land \operatorname{Even}(n)\,\},\\[2pt]
&\; \forall i\in\{1,\dots,len(out)-1\}\;:\; out[i] < out[i+1].
\end{aligned}}
\]

Both formulations guarantee that the output consists of **all** even numbers in the interval \([1,100]\), each appearing **once**, and that they appear in **ascending order**, one per line.

---

### Hoare triple  

Putting the pre‑ and post‑conditions together, the specification of the whole program is the Hoare triple  

\[
\boxed{\{\,\text{true}\,\}\;\; \text{Program}\;\; \{\,Q\,\}}
\]

where `Program` is any implementation that writes the sequence `out` satisfying the predicate `Q` above.
