**Hoare‑logic specification**

We describe the program only by its pre‑condition (what is known about the
input) and its post‑condition (what must hold for the three numbers that are
printed).  
All auxiliary variables that the program may use are quantified in the
post‑condition, therefore the specification is *purely declarative*.

---

### 1.   Vocabulary  

* `L = ⟨l₁ , … , l_n⟩` – the whole input, a finite sequence of *tokens*.  

* `B = { b ∈ {'‑','*'}³ }` – the set of **blink** tokens (three characters,
each either ‘‑’ or ‘*’).  

* `S = { “caw caw” }` – the unique **scream** token.  

* `val : B → ℕ` – conversion of a blink into a decimal number, interpreting
‘‑’ as binary 0 and ‘*’ as binary 1, most‑significant bit on the left:

\[
\begin{aligned}
\text{val}(b_1b_2b_3) &=
\mathbf 1_{b_1='*'}\;2^{2}
+ \mathbf 1_{b_2='*'}\;2^{1}
+ \mathbf 1_{b_3='*'}\;2^{0} \\[2mm]
&\qquad\bigl(\mathbf 1_{P}=1\text{ if }P\text{ true, }0\text{ otherwise}\bigr)
\end{aligned}
\]

* `g(i) = |\{ j\le i \mid l_j\in S\}|` – number of screams that appear **up to**
position `i`.  Thus `g(i)=k` means that the token `l_i` lies in the
*(k+1)‑st* group of blinks (the groups are delimited by screams).

* `R₁ , R₂ , R₃ ∈ ℕ` – the three results that the program must output.

---

### 2.   Pre‑condition  

\[
\boxed{
\begin{array}{l}
\text{Pre} \equiv
\bigl(L = \langle l_1,\dots ,l_n\rangle\ \wedge\ 
\forall i\;(1\le i\le n\;\Rightarrow\; l_i\in B\ \lor\ l_i\in S)\bigr)
\\[1mm]
\text{(the input consists only of blink tokens and the token “caw caw”).}
\end{array}}
\]

No further assumption is required – the program must work for *any*
finite sequence of such tokens (there may be 0, 1, 2 or 3 screams, any
order).

---

### 3.   Post‑condition  

For each \(k\in\{1,2,3\}\) the result \(R_k\) equals the sum of the
numeric values of all blinks that belong to the *k‑th* group (i.e. the
blinks that appear after the \((k-1)^{\text{st}}\) scream and before the
\(k^{\text{th}}\) scream).  If a group does not exist, its sum is defined
to be 0.

\[
\boxed{
\begin{aligned}
\text{Post} \equiv\;&
\bigl(R_1,R_2,R_3\bigr)\text{ are printed (one per line)}\\[1mm]
&\wedge\;
\forall k\in\{1,2,3\}\;:\;
R_k = \sum_{i=1}^{n}
\Bigl[\,l_i\in B\ \wedge\ g(i)=k-1\,\Bigr]\;\cdot\;\text{val}(l_i)
\end{aligned}}
\]

The bracket \([P]\) is the Iverson‑bracket (1 if the predicate \(P\) holds,
0 otherwise).  Consequently:

* `R₁` is the sum of the values of all blinks occurring **before the first
  “caw caw”**,
* `R₂` is the sum of the values of all blinks occurring **between the first
  and the second “caw caw”**,
* `R₃` is the sum of the values of all blinks occurring **between the second
  and the third “caw caw”**.

If fewer than three groups appear, the corresponding sums are empty and
therefore equal to 0, which matches the required output in the examples.

---

### 4.   Hoare triple  

Putting the two parts together we obtain the formal specification of the
program:

\[
\boxed{\;\{\,\text{Pre}\,\}\; \text{Program} \; \{\,\text{Post}\,\}\;}
\]

Any implementation that, when started in a state satisfying *Pre*, terminates
and leaves the state satisfying *Post* (i.e. prints the three numbers
\(R_1,R_2,R_3\)) is correct with respect to the problem description.
