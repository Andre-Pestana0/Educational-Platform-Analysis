**Hoare‑style specification**

Let  

* \(N\in \mathbb N\) be the input variable (the number of repetitions).  
* \(a_0 = 3\) and \(a_1 = 6\) – the coefficients of the periodic continued fraction for \(\sqrt{10}\).  

Define a helper sequence \((I_k)_{k\ge 0}\) that builds the “inner” continued fraction:

\[
\begin{aligned}
I_0 &:= a_1 ,\\[2mm]
I_{k+1} &:= a_1 + \frac{1}{I_k}\qquad (k\ge 0).
\end{aligned}
\]

Using this sequence we obtain the value of the continued fraction after exactly \(N\) repetitions:

\[
\operatorname{CF}(N) \;:=\;
\begin{cases}
a_0, & N = 0,\\[2mm]
a_0 + \displaystyle\frac{1}{I_N}, & N\ge 1 .
\end{cases}
\tag{1}
\]

The required output is the decimal representation of \(\operatorname{CF}(N)\) **rounded to ten fractional digits**.  
We denote this rounding operation by  

\[
\operatorname{round}_{10}(x) \;:=\; \text{the unique decimal number } y \text{ such that } 
|y-x| \le \tfrac{1}{2}\,10^{-10}
\text{ and } y \text{ has exactly }10\text{ digits after the point.}
\]

---

### Hoare triple

\[
\boxed{\{\,0\le N\le 100\,\}\;\; \texttt{contFracSqrt}(N)\;\; \{\,\texttt{out}= \operatorname{round}_{10}(\operatorname{CF}(N))\;\land\;
\texttt{out}\text{ is a decimal string with exactly 10 digits after the point}\,\}}
\]

---

### Explanation of the post‑condition

* **\( \operatorname{CF}(N) \)** is the exact real value obtained from the periodic continued fraction  

  \[
  a_0+\cfrac{1}{\displaystyle a_1+\cfrac{1}{\displaystyle a_1+\cfrac{1}{\displaystyle a_1+\dots}}}
  \]

  where the denominator block “\(a_1\)” appears **\(N\)** times after the initial term \(a_0\).

* **\( \operatorname{round}_{10}(\operatorname{CF}(N)) \)** is that real value rounded to the nearest multiple of \(10^{-10}\); the rounding rule is the usual “round‑half‑up”.

* The predicate “\(\texttt{out}\) is a decimal string with exactly 10 digits after the point’’ guarantees that the program prints the result in the required format, e.g.  

  * \(N=0 \;\Rightarrow\; \texttt{out}=3.0000000000\)  
  * \(N=1 \;\Rightarrow\; \texttt{out}=3.1666666667\)  
  * \(N=5 \;\Rightarrow\; \texttt{out}=3.1622776623\).

Thus the Hoare triple above completely captures the required **pre‑condition** (input range) and **post‑condition** (exact numeric result and output format) of the problem.
