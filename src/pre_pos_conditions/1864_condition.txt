Below is a **Hoare‑logic style specification** for a program that solves the problem.  
Only the *pre‑condition* (what must be true before the program starts) and the *post‑condition* (what must be true after it finishes) are given; the body of the program is omitted.

---

### 1.  Notation

* Let  

\[
\mathbb N = \{0,1,2,\dots\}
\]

* The input variable is  

\[
N \in \mathbb N .
\]

* The **alphabet** allowed in the output is  

\[
\Sigma = \{\,\text{‘A’},\text{‘B’},\dots ,\text{‘Z’},\text{‘ ’}\,\}.
\]

* The **quote** that must be used (the “underlined letters” of the statement) is a **fixed** string of length  

\[
|S| = 34 .
\]

We denote it by an indexed sequence of characters

\[
S = s_{0}s_{1}\dots s_{33},\qquad s_{i}\in\Sigma .
\]

(The concrete values of the \(s_i\) are known to the judge; they are not needed for the specification itself.  
From the examples we know that the prefix of length 7 is “LIFE IS”.)

* For any string \(x\) and any integer \(k\) with \(0\le k\le |x|\) we write  

\[
\operatorname{pref}_{k}(x)=x[0..k-1]
\]

for the *prefix* consisting of the first \(k\) characters of \(x\).

* The program writes its result on the standard output.  
We denote the **output string** (including the final end‑of‑line character) by  

\[
\mathit{out}.
\]

* The end‑of‑line character is denoted by **`\(\nl\)`**; it is **not** counted in the length of the visible part of the output.

---

### 2.  Hoare Triple

\[
\boxed{\;\{\,P(N)\,\}\;\; \textbf{program}\;\; \{\,Q(N,\mathit{out})\,\}\;}
\]

---

#### Pre‑condition  \(P(N)\)

\[
P(N) \;\equiv\; N\in\mathbb N\;\land\;1\le N\le 34 .
\]

The only requirement before execution is that the input integer \(N\) is in the domain prescribed by the statement.

---

#### Post‑condition  \(Q(N,\mathit{out})\)

\[
\begin{aligned}
Q(N,\mathit{out})\;\equiv\;
&\bigl(\mathit{out}= \operatorname{pref}_{N}(S)\;\Vert\;\nl\bigr) \\[2mm]
&\land\; |\operatorname{pref}_{N}(S)| = N \\[1mm]
&\land\; \forall i\;(0\le i < N \;\Rightarrow\; s_{i}\in\Sigma) .
\end{aligned}
\]

In words:

1. **Exact prefix** – the visible part of the output is precisely the first \(N\) characters of the constant quote \(S\).  
   The output is terminated by a single newline character `\(\nl\)`.

2. **Length** – the visible part has length exactly \(N\).

3. **Alphabet** – every character that appears in the visible part belongs to the allowed alphabet \(\Sigma\) (capital letters or a blank space).  

Because the newline is not counted in the length, the specification guarantees that the line printed to the screen consists *only* of capital letters and blanks, exactly as required.

---

### 3.  Full Specification in Hoare‑logic Form

\[
\boxed{
\begin{array}{l}
\{\,1\le N\le 34\,\}\;\; \textbf{read }N;\\[1mm]
\qquad\textbf{write } \operatorname{pref}_{N}(S)\;\nl;\\[1mm]
\{\,\mathit{out}= \operatorname{pref}_{N}(S)\;\Vert\;\nl\;\land\;|\operatorname{pref}_{N}(S)|=N\,\}
\end{array}
}
\]

The body of the program (the two statements “read \(N\)” and “write …”) is just a convenient way to express the same pre‑/post‑conditions; the essential contract is the pair \((P,Q)\) given above.
